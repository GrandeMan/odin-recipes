Pizza Dough

Description

Your pizza is only as good as its dough. The dough literally makes or breaks the pizza which is why it's important to get it right. Luckily, dough is generally hard to mess up. If you add too much water, you can add flour and vice versa, but, there are ingredients and methods that change the textures and flavours of your dough. I've tried different methods and this is the one I almost always use. I do everything by hand (like some sort of purist), but you can just as easily use a food processor or stand mixer. It does not affect the end product and makes the process a bit easier.

Ingredients

  • 470 grams/3 3/4 cups of bread flour, 25 grams of sugar
  • 25 grams/1/4 cup of sugar
  • 1 1/2 grams/ 1/2 teaspoon yeast
  • 285 grams/1 1/4 cups ice water (use a strainer to catch ice cubes)
  • 10 grams/2 teaspoons kosher salt
  • 15 grams/1 tablespoon olive oil

Steps

  1. Combine flour, sugar, and yeast in a bowl and mix with a spoon to combine everything.
  2. Add a bit of water and mix, repeat until a ball of dough forms. You're aiming for a dough that's about 60% hydrated.
    3. Don't panic if your dough is a little sticky, this is what we want. However, if you add a bit too much water then add more flour.
  3. After letting the dough rest for about 15 minutes, add the salt and oil then mix (with your tool of choice, for me it's a spoon) until you have a smooth, supple, and silky dough [usually about 2 mins]
  4. Using a lightly oiled countertop and hands, knead the dough until it cohesively comes together in a very soft and smooth mass. Form it into a taut ball and place it into an oiled container to proof in the fridge overnight.
    5. The longer you leave the dough to develop, the better the texture and flavour. Overnight is ideal and you can also leave it for much longer, but if you're pressed for time 2 hours at room temp works.
  5. Split the dough into 2 parts and roll them into balls. Place the dough on a lightly floured rimmed baking sheet or proofing box at room temperature until it is light and easy to work with.

*This recipe makes enough dough for 2 pizzas*
